Chicken Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a popular meal throughout West Africa known for being fairly simple to make while being packed with flavor. The recipe can be changed around to reflect whatever happens to be seasonal in your area. It's only real staple is rice. Once you master it you can easily make your own versions. Credit for this recipe goes to Oumou from recettesafricaine.com

Ingredients

  • 1 Kg jasmin rice or any long grain rice
  • 1 kg Chicken
  • 1 1/2 l of water
  • 250 ml vegetable oil
  • 2 onions
  • 1 bunch of green onions
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato concentrate
  • Parsley and Basil
  • A couple mint leaves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1/2 a teaspoon of thyme and rosemary
  • Some chili peppers
  • your choice of vegetables: potatoes, carrots, bell peppers...etc. (Nothing that would become soggy though)
  • Salt, pepper, and an optional bouillon or stock cube.

    • Steps

    1. Put the rice in a big bowl and pour hot water over it, add salt and a bay leaf. Let rest for 20 min then wash the rice until the water runs clear
    2. Season the chicken with salt and pepper then fry it in a large pot with some vegetable oil until golden. Remove and put to the side
    3. In a food processor blend onions, green onions, parsley, basil, mint leaves, bell pepper, chili peppers, and the cloves of garlic.
    4. Crush the tomatoes in a mortar and add the concentrate along with half of the onion blend
    5. In a large pot heat some vegetable oil and add the tomato blend to fry off for a couple minutes. While the blend is frying add your vegetables and a cup of water. Let simmer until chicken and vegetables are cooked
    6. Remove chicken and cooked vegetales put on the side
    7. Add the water the rest on the onion blend and bring to a boil.
    8. Add the rice and cook on medium heat covered until fully cooked stirring occasionally to avoid sticking. If its too hot lower the heat
    9. Once the rice is cooked serve with the vegetables and chicken
